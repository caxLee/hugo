MIT Professor Emeritus Samuel Jay Keyser's new book 'Play It Again, Sam' delves into the cognitive impact of repetition in art, using the 1953 jazz standard 'Satin Doll' as a prime example. Keyser explores how repetition, such as the 'same/except' construction, enhances the artistic experience in music, poetry, painting, and photography. Inspired by the Margulis experiment, Keyser investigates how encountering repetition triggers pleasure in audiences and potentially affects brain activity, aiming to understand the connection between repetition and endorphin release.
