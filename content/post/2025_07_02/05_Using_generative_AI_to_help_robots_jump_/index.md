title = 'Using generative AI to help robots jump higher and land safely'
+++

Diffusion models like OpenAI’s DALL-E are being used to generate new designs by prompting AI systems to create images, videos, or blueprints. Generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) models are also creating working robots by generating structures and control systems. MIT’s CSAIL developed a system where users can draft a 3D model of a robot, specify parts to modify, and use GenAI to optimize the design, resulting in a robot that jumps higher. The AI-generated design featured curved linkages, improving the robot's performance. The researchers optimized the design through multiple iterations, achieving significant improvements in jumping and landing abilities. The use of diffusion models in robot design could revolutionize how machines are created, with potential applications in various industries. Future iterations could incorporate lighter materials and more complex functionalities, paving the way for innovative robotics designs guided by generative AI.
