Es Devlin, recipient of the 2025 Eugene McDermott Award in the Arts at MIT, is known for creating immersive environments that bring people together in collective experiences. Her diverse body of work ranges from designing for large-scale events like the London Olympics and Super Bowl halftime shows to intimate projects such as drawing strangers' portraits. Devlin's recent project at MIT, Congregation, involved participants drawing each other in silence to foster connection and understanding. Through her work, she explores themes of self-discovery, empathy, and unity, emphasizing the importance of transcending individual boundaries to build meaningful connections with others. Devlin's artistry reflects a deep engagement with the human experience and a commitment to bridging divides through shared creativity and expression.
