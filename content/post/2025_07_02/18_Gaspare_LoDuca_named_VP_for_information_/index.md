Gaspare LoDuca has been appointed as MIT’s vice president for information systems and technology (IS&T) and chief information officer, effective Aug. 18. He brings over two decades of IT leadership experience in higher education and succeeds Mark Silis. LoDuca will lead IS&T, oversee MIT’s IT infrastructure, and play a key role in modernizing business processes. As a collaborative leader, he aims to shape IT policy and strategy at MIT and support the community's needs. LoDuca's background includes overseeing IT at Columbia University and managing technology strategies at Accenture. He holds a bachelor's degree in chemical engineering and is relocating to Boston with his wife.
